Kiran Bedi - Biography

First Woman IPS Officer of India

Introduction

Kiran Bedi (born 9 June 1949) is an Indian social activist and former police officer. She became the first woman officer to join the Indian Police Service (IPS) in 1972. She served the country for 35 years and later worked in social service and politics. She also served as the Lieutenant Governor of :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1} from 2016 to 2021.

Kiran Bedi Biography

Early Life

Kiran Bedi was born in Amritsar, Punjab, in a Punjabi family. Her parents always supported education and sports. From childhood she was very active and hardworking.

h2>Education

Kiran Bedi completed her Bachelor’s degree in English from Government College for Women, Amritsar. She later completed a Master’s degree in Political Science from Panjab University. She also completed Law from Delhi University and PhD from IIT Delhi.

Tennis Career

Kiran Bedi started playing tennis at the age of nine. She became National Junior Tennis Champion in 1966. She won many championships between 1965 and 1978.

IPS Career

She joined IPS in 1972 and worked in Delhi, Goa, Chandigarh and Mizoram. She became famous for discipline and public reforms.

Tihar Jail Reforms

Kiran Bedi introduced many reforms in Tihar Jail such as education programs, meditation, and skill development for prisoners. Her work made the jail environment more positive.
